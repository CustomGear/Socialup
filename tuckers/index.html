Our Story

It began with Mother Tucker.

Mother Tucker — Mama Lisa, as the canal workers affectionately called her — was a remarkable woman who played a vital role in the construction of Ottawa's Rideau Canal. Born in Ireland in 1789, she immigrated to Canada with her husband John Tucker, a carpenter, in 1816, settling in Bytown and opening a boarding house near the canal site.

She cooked for Colonel By's men — hearty stews, fresh bread, hot tea — and offered comfort to workers facing harsh conditions far from home. She was a mother figure to many, and her table was always full.

Two centuries later, the spirit of that table lives on at 61 York Street: generous portions, honest cooking, and a seat for everyone.
