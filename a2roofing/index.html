The honest verdict

The website looks significantly better than it performs as a marketing asset.

It has strong branding, modern visuals and a credible premium feel — but it's currently structured more like a six-page brochure than a website designed to generate roofing leads through Google. It's built in Webflow, which means most of what's below can be fixed without a rebuild.

—

Current Google visibility

Branded searches

Google can find the homepage and contact page when someone searches specifically for "A2 Roofing" or the domain. But the brand is presented inconsistently across the site:

A2 Roofing Asquare Roofing Asquare Team A2

That inconsistency makes it harder for Google to understand the company as a single, clear business entity.

Non-branded searches

In the public search results reviewed on July 22, 2026, A2 did not surface among the leading organic results for:

Roofing company Ottawa Roofing contractor Ottawa Roof repair Ottawa Flat roofing Ottawa Commercial roofing Ottawa Emergency roof repair Ottawa Roof inspection Ottawa Roofing company Kanata Roofing company Cornwall Roofing company Kingston

Those results were dominated by companies such as Roofmaster, Firon, Sanderson, Wolfenburg, Total Roofing, RoofLife and Morin Roofing.

Ranking positions shift by location, device and personalization — exact rankings would require Search Console or a postal-code-level tracker. But this snapshot strongly suggests little meaningful non-branded organic visibility today.

Website architecture

Too few pages

The site currently has six public pages: Home, Solutions, FAQ, About, Contact, Privacy Policy. The homepage lists five cities and a dozen-plus services — but none of them has a properly optimized standalone page.

One generic "Solutions" page is trying to rank for flat roofing, TPO, EPDM, elastomeric membrane, metal roofing, standing seam, steel shingles, asphalt shingles, inspections, emergency repair, residential, commercial — across five cities. Google needs a clear page per search intent; one page can't realistically compete for all of it.

Recommended structure

At minimum, dedicated pages for:

Roofing Contractor Ottawa Roof Repair Ottawa Emergency Roof Repair Ottawa Flat Roofing Ottawa Commercial Roofing Ottawa Metal Roofing Ottawa Asphalt Shingle Roofing Ottawa Roof Inspections Ottawa TPO Roofing Ottawa EPDM Roofing Ottawa Elastomeric Membrane Roofing Ottawa

Then location pages only for cities you can genuinely support with projects, testimonials and photos. Strongest first: Ottawa, Kanata, Nepean, Orleans, Barrhaven, Stittsville, Gatineau. Brockville, Cornwall and Kingston can follow — but each already has locally established competitors, so simply listing the city on the homepage won't compete against businesses with addresses, reviews and dedicated pages.

Heading structure

The heading hierarchy is irregular — the homepage appears to use several H1-level headings instead of one clear primary H1, About and Contact use oversized blocks as H1s, and the Solutions page opens with generic secondary headings instead of a keyword-focused page heading.

Better structure:

H1 — Roofing Services in Ottawa

H2 — Flat Roofing Systems

H3 — TPO Roofing

H3 — EPDM Roofing

H3 — Elastomeric Membrane Roofing

One H1 per page, H2s for major sections, H3s for individual products or questions — so both Google and visitors understand what the page is actually about.

For a company selling projects worth thousands of dollars, this quietly erodes trust and conversion.

Local SEO & Google Business Profile

This may be the biggest immediate weakness.

No prominent Google Business Profile could be verified for either "A2 Roofing" or "Asquare Roofing" in public brand searches — which could mean it doesn't exist, is under a different name, is new/weak, has inconsistent information, or simply isn't ranking. The site names Alexandre Langlois and an 819 number, while the Ottawa address only appears inside the privacy policy rather than on the Contact page or footer.

Fixes

Standardize the exact same business name, phone number, address/service-area, URL, hours and description across: Google Business Profile, website, Bing Places, Apple Business Connect, BBB, HomeStars, manufacturer directories, local associations and social profiles. If customers don't visit the Ottawa address, the Google profile should be configured as a service-area business rather than displaying a residential address.

Phone number: an 819 number isn't harmful, especially serving Gatineau — but an Ottawa homeowner may trust a 613 number more. Keep 819, add a trackable 613 for marketing.

The About page says A2 has served Ottawa "for many years" with no founding year or factual history. Competitors lead heavily with proof: Roofmaster promotes 40+ years, ~25,000 projects, $5M liability insurance, WSIB coverage and 300+ reviews. Firon has dedicated review, award and certification pages. Sanderson promotes 125+ years and BBB recognition.

Conversion optimization

The site is attractive, but the sales message is too vague.

Current hero

"We build high-performing roofing solutions for Residential & Commercial" — CTA: "Discover more"

Doesn't communicate where they operate, what problem they solve, why they're different, or what to do next.

Better direction

"Ottawa Roofing Built to Withstand Canadian Weather" — Residential and commercial roof installation, repairs and emergency service across Ottawa and Eastern Ontario. CTA: "Get a Free Roofing Estimate | Call 24/7"

Beneath it: 10-Year Workmanship Warranty • Fully Insured • Residential & Commercial • Emergency Service — only claims that can be documented.

Elements to add

  • Google star rating in the hero
  • Sticky mobile "Call Now" button
  • Short quote form above the fold
  • Separate emergency roofing CTA
  • Project gallery + before/after photos
  • Warranty explanation, insurance & certification badges
  • Service-area map, financing info if available
  • Real photos of the crew and vehicles
  • Case studies by city, roofing system and outcome

Content strategy

There's no visible blog or educational resource section — leaving high-intent searches uncontested:

How much does a new roof cost in Ottawa? How long do asphalt shingles last? TPO vs EPDM roofing Signs of an ice dam What to do when your roof leaks Does re-roofing need a permit in Ottawa? Metal vs asphalt shingles How much snow can an Ottawa roof hold? Insurance after storm damage

Firon, Sanderson and High 5 Exteriors already publish locally relevant content that strengthens their service pages. A2 shouldn't publish generic AI filler — every article should answer a real homeowner question, reference local conditions, link to a service page, and ideally use photos from an actual A2 project.

Technical SEO

Working in your favor

  • HTTPS active
  • Short, readable URLs
  • Primary content is crawlable
  • Webflow foundation is easy to maintain
  • Already being discovered by search engines

Needs backend access to verify

Search Console ownership, GA4, form & call tracking, XML sitemap, robots.txt, canonical tags, redirects, broken links, custom 404, image sizes, Core Web Vitals, mobile PageSpeed, LocalBusiness/Service/FAQ/Breadcrumb schema, duplicate hidden content, full indexing status.

We wouldn't call the site "slow" without running PageSpeed Insights directly — the design uses large photography and effects, so image/animation optimization should be tested, not assumed.

You don't need to throw the website away. You need an SEO and lead-generation layer built onto the branding you've already invested in: a larger service-page architecture, local authority, reviews and ongoing project content — not just a title-tag cleanup.
